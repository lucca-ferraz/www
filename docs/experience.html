Experience

Tampa Bay Rays

Baseball Research & Development Intern

January - August 2025

During my internship with the Rays, I focused on building tools and models to support player evaluation and decision-making:

  • I developed an R Shiny application that automatically identified concerning amateur pitchers, streamlining the scouting process and saving the staff 100+ hours of manual tagging.

  • I trained an XGBoost model to evaluate and predict baserunning reads on more than 150,000 batted balls dating back to 2015, helping quantify a previously hard-to-measure skill.

  • I created an automated RMarkdown reporting system for park factor corrections, improving the accuracy of draft prospect evaluations across different playing environments.

These projects combined statistical modeling, data engineering, and visualization to deliver tools that directly supported the Rays’ front office and scouting operations.

Rice Football

Student Head of Analytics

August 2022 - Present

I’ve been part of Rice Football since my very first week of college, starting as a video operations intern filming practices and games for the coaching staff. After one semester, I transitioned into recruiting and scouting, where I evaluated prospects through extensive video review and led official/unofficial visits for recruits and their families.

By my sophomore year, I helped build out Rice’s first student-led analytics department, which has since grown from just 2 people to a team of 12. In this role, I’ve led projects that directly supported coaches and recruiting staff, including:

  • Developing analytics-based scouting reports for weekly opponents.

  • Creating machine learning models to project transfer portal entries and estimate the likelihood of players transferring to Rice.

  • Designing a R Shiny web application to automate opponent scouting processes.

  • Assisting in the evaluation of every Texas Class of 2024 high school prospect.

This experience has given me hands-on exposure to multiple aspects of a Division I football program (video operations, recruiting, scouting, and analytics) while also allowing me to lead and mentor a growing team of student analysts.

Carnegie Mellon University

Undergraduate Researcher - Department of Statistics and Data Science

June - August 2024

I was one of 15 students selected for a fully-funded 8-week research program at Carnegie Mellon’s Department of Statistics & Data Science, where I applied advanced statistical methods to problems in sports analytics.

My capstone project, “Batted Passes in the NFL: A Hierarchical Approach to Explaining Variance of an Unlikely Event” (advised by Dr. Ron Yurko), investigated the drivers behind one of football’s rarest but impactful plays. Using a mixed-effects logistic regression model, I:

  • Quantified how play context, quarterback tendencies, and defensive pressure contribute to batted passes.

  • Generated quarterback rankings based on random effect estimates of their ability to avoid batted passes.

  • Produced a full paper, poster, and presentation, which I presented at the 2024 Carnegie Mellon Sports Analytics Conference.

This experience deepened my skills in hierarchical modeling, statistical inference, and communicating results to both technical and non-technical audiences.
